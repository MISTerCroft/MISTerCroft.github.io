How to Buy from a Vending Machine

By Josh Croft


Lab 3: iFrames and Widgets
Step 1: Obtain a Program Application

There are two ways to get started! The fastest way to get the application to us is by printing it out. Select the application that pertains to your age.

You might also get an application from a recruiter from the CA Decoy Program.

There is a chance you heard about this from a local group like a school or youth organization, and they might have out applications too. Check in with who shared the Decoy Program and ask them for an application packet, they might have one.

Step 2: Fill in Black Ink Only

This is a very important task you're helping with. The forms must be completed in a specific way to be complete. Using black ink is important to the teams taking you out.

Find a pen with black ink and fill out all the sections on the application pages.

If you cannot find a pen, refer to option '2' on the website.

Step 3: Make Sure Guardians Sign Documents

Take the pen you used, and have your parent or guardian fill out the required areas.

There are 3 locations for them to sign.

It is very important that the consent signatures are in black ink.

Step 4: Check the Application for Completion

Scan the areas where you should sign. As you come across areas that are blank, make sure it's filled in.

Use 'N/A' (Not Applicable) in the blank spaces you don't need to put information in. Even though you're not filling out all spaces with info, make sure N/A is in places you would leave blank.

There are 3 pages to fill out in total. Make sure YOU fill out all areas and that PARENTS or GUARDIANS fill out their 3 required sections.

Step 5: Put the Address on the Envelope

All applications come stapled to an envelope. It may have the address on it already, but if it doesn't then please write the following:

540 N Augusta St. Fresno, CA 93701

Write this address in the middle of the envelope.

Remember to write a return address in the top left corner.

Put a stamp on the top right corner of the envelope.

Step 6: Fold the Pages and Seal the Envelope

Take the 3 pages you and your parents filled out and fold them into thirds, or three equal parts. They will fit snug into the envelope.

Seal the envelope shut with the papers inside.

Step 7: Send the Application in the Mail!

Ask your parent where the mail pick-up location is. They should be able to help you get the envelope in the right place to be sent.

If you got the application from a local youth organization, they will probably be able to help you mail it off as well. It never hurts to ask and you'll learn this for the future!
