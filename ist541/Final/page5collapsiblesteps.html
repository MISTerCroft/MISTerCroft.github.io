Steps to Assemble

Depending on how many places you need watered, you may need to repeat that step for your specific project.


Follow these steps
Step 1: Measuring First

Measure the distance from your faucet to the end of your watering area. Add two feet to your measurement, and that is how much half inch pipe you'll need.

In this example, we are measuring a garden area.

We measured 40 feet from the spigot to the end of the flowerbed, and we are going to add 2 feet to that. So we will use 42 feet of 1/2 inch tubing. Cut your line with scissors to the length you need.

If you have 2 shorter lines, use the brown connectors to make the full length!

 

Step 2: Assemble Backflow Preventer

Attach the backflow preventer to the spigot and assemble it as shown in the photo.

 

Step 3: Run and Anchor the Main Line

Attach the Backflow Preventer to the main hose line. This part is a little tricky, but unscrew the endcap and put the endcap on the hoseline. Then push the hose onto the module's nozzle, push really hard. Then screw the cap on to keep the hose locked on.

Lay the hose out and push in stakes to hold the main line in place.

 

Step 4: Prepping direct watering lines

Measure the distance from the main tube to the base of the plant.

Cut the 1/4 inch tubing to fit each watering spot. See the video below for reference.

You will be connecting these lines to the connectors and nozzles.

 

Step 5: Puncture and Attach Connector

Take the purple tool and use it to place the connector where you want it.

You can do this step all at once. Or you can do Step 6 right after, and repeat the two at each drip location.

 

Step 6: Push Small Line in Place

Attach the small line onto the connector.

Attach the nozzle to the other end.

Use the stand attachment to lift the nozzle if needed. It will prevent dirt and debris from getting in and lessen the chances of critters clogging the nozzle.

 

Step 7: Clamp The End

After attaching all your 1/4 inch lines, put the clamp attachment on at the end of the drip line.

Fold the end of the pipe back as desired and slide the clampover the tubing at the other end.

You could also plug the end for extra leakage protection, but this tubing is designed to fully clamp and stop when folded.

Our goal with this project is to save water in general so the added clamp will ensure we meet that goal.
