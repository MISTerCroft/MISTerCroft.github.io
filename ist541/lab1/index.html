How to Buy from a Vending Machine

By Josh Croft


Lab 1: Job Aid
Step 1: Select Visually the Item of Choice

Scan through the different snacks and check whether there is something that might satisfy your cravings. The types of products that you can buy vary from machine to machine; however, most of them include some kind of chips, chocolates, cookies, and gums. Some machines offer healthy snacks such as baked chips, granola bars, and peanuts.

Even though most vending machines contain fresh products, it might be convenient to try to visually double check the expiration date of the snack you want to buy.

Also, it is important to inspect visually whether the item of choice is all the way to the front in the dispensing coil, otherwise, the item will not be push out when the coil spins.

Step 2: Get the Item of Choice Identification Code

Once you have selected visually the item you want to buy, get its identification code. This code is located right below the item.

In some vending machines this code is composed of a letter and a number like "C5"; however in some other machines, it is just a number.

If for any reason the identification code is not visible (e.g., the snack itself is covering part of the code), try looking at the codes next to it (to the left, right, above, and below). In most vending machines the codes follow a sequence and by looking at the codes next to one item, it is possible to deduce its code.

Remember this identification code since you'll be using it in the next steps.

Step 3: Check the Item of Choice Price

Most vending machines provides to ways to check for the item price. In some of them, the price is located right below the item, next to the identification code.

In other machines, you need to enter the identification code by pushing the corresponding buttons and the price will be shown on a display, like the one shown on the picture to the right.

Step 4: Pay for the Item of Choice

To pay for the item of choice, most vending machines accept coins (5, 10, and 25 cents) and $1 bills. Some machines will accept $5 bills.

Newer vending machines also accept credit cards.

If paying with cash, usually you don't need to worry about paying the exact amount since most machines provide change. However, some machines do have an indicator (usually a red light) that will tell you whether the machine is capable of giving you change at that time.

Step 5: Enter the Item of Choice Identification Code

Most vending machines have buttons with numbers or letters. Uou need to press these buttons to enter the identification code of the item of choice.

Once you enter the identification code, press the "Okay" or "Enter" button.

In machines that require you to enter the identification code in order to check the price, you would have already done this step earlier.

Step 6: Grab the Purchased Item

After you pay for the item, the dispensing coil should spin and push the item out, so the item will fall to the dispensing area. Open the door of the dispensing area, which should below the snacks and grab the item.

Some times the item gets stuck in the dispensing coil and would not fall down. Most machines have a phone number where you can report the incident.

Step 7: Get your Change

Check whether the machine gives you any change for the item purchased.
